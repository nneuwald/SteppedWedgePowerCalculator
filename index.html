ICC (stability of a person\'s outcome across repeated assessments)

What it means: if you measure the same person at two timepoints, how correlated are their scores? High ICC (0.50+) = stable outcomes within person. Low ICC = scores fluctuate a lot.


How to estimate from pilot data (R):

icc(data, model="twoway", type="consistency", unit="single")

Use control-phase data only so the treatment effect doesn’t bias the estimate. Use the lowest pairwise ICC (e.g. baseline–24 months) as your primary estimate — most conservative. Report the average as a sensitivity analysis.


Typical ranges:

Body weight / BMI0.70–0.90
HbA1c / glycemic markers0.50–0.75
Behavioral outcomes0.20–0.50
Psychological / self-report0.30–0.60
Between-cluster ICC (sites)0.01–0.10

Direction: for within-subject designs, higher ICC = more power (stable variance cancels out). For between-subject designs, ICC has less impact.
